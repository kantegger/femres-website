Early summer in a remote village in northern Turkey. Lale and her four sisters walk home from school, innocently splashing in the sea with some boys. However, this pure joy is twisted into an unforgivable scandal of "debauchery" in the eyes of the conservative villagers.

The girls' home is swiftly transformed into a prison built of traditional morality. Phones are cut, doors are locked, and their laughter-filled school life is replaced by dreary homemaking training. Their grandmother and uncle begin arranging marriages for them, preparing to "marry them off" one by one like commodities.

Faced with suffocating patriarchal oppression, these five young "mustangs" do not choose submission. They build a line of defense with their intimacy and batter the antiquated system with their youthful restlessness and desire. Although their struggle moves from playful entanglement to desperate escape, involves compromise and sacrifice, that primal vitality seeking freedom continues to burn fiercely in the stifling air. Even at a painful cost, they are determined to kick down the fences and run towards their own wilderness.
