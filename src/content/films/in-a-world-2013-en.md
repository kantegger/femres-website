Lake Bell’s *In a World...* (2013) is a witty and perceptive exploration of the literal and figurative marginalization of women’s voices within the entertainment industry. Bell, pulling quadruple duty as writer, director, producer, and star, uses the niche, hyper-masculine enclave of movie trailer voice-overs as a piercing metaphor for the broader systemic barriers—the "glass ceiling" of the sound booth—that women face. The narrative follows Carol Solomon, an overlooked vocal coach with a husky, unrefined cadence, as she attempts to break into a field long dominated by "In a world..." patriarchs like her own father, the legendary and dismissive Sam Sotto (Fred Melamed). Through Carol’s journey, Bell deconstructs how cultural authority is historically coded as masculine, exposing the subtle gatekeeping that deems women’s voices as either too "sexy" or too "nasal" to command the public stage.

A particularly resonant aspect of the film is its critique of the "sexy baby voice"—the infantilized, high-pitched vocal trend that Bell identifies as a symptom of female self-erasure in patriarchal society. By teaching her female students to reclaim their natural registers, Carol acts as more than a speech therapist; she is a political agitator for female gravitas. The film’s brilliance lies in its ability to balance sharp industry satire with deep psychological insight into the gendered nature of mentorship. The character of Sam Sotto encapsulates the "benevolent patriarchy"—a man who loves his daughter but cannot conceive of her as a peer or a competitor. By positioning Carol’s breakthrough in direct opposition to her father and his chosen male protégé, the narrative illustrates that for women to find their "true voice," they must often dismantle the very legacies that raised them.

The film's meta-narrative is equally compelling; Bell’s own "quadruple duty" behind and in front of the camera mirrors Carol’s struggle for creative control. The film also tackles the "sisterhood" dilemma, showing how internalized sexism can pit women against each other for the scraps of male attention, before ultimately advocating for a collective reclamation of female vocal agency. The inclusion of Geena Davis—a real-life advocate for gender balance in media—in a cameo role underscores the film’s status as a serious manifesto wrapped in the skin of a lighthearted comedy.

Ultimately, *In a World...* is a celebratory anthem for the "husky-voiced" woman and every individual who has been told they don't sound like "authority." It achieves the rare feat of being a crowd-pleasing comedy that never sacrifices its feminist convictions, reminding audiences that representation begins with who gets to tell the story—and whose voice we hear in our ears when the lights go down. By centering a woman’s professional triumph as the ultimate romantic and personal payoff, Bell’s debut feature serves as both a critique of the status quo and a blueprint for a more inclusive creative future, where the phrase "In a world..." is finally followed by a woman’s own name.
